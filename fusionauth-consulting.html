WHY FUSIONAUTH

FusionAuth is what enterprise identity should be: full-featured, developer-friendly, and priced fairly.

  • Full SSO/MFA feature set: SAML, OIDC, OAuth 2.0, MFA, user management - everything Okta does.
  • Self-hosting option: Your data stays on your servers. Full control.
  • Community Edition is free: No per-user fees. Run it on your infrastructure.
  • Paid plans are reasonable: When you need support or advanced features, the pricing doesn't scale absurdly.
  • Developer-friendly: Clean APIs, good docs, actually enjoyable to work with.

The catch? It's not Okta's one-click setup. You need to know what you're doing. That's where I come in.

WHAT RESULTS LOOK LIKE

SAN of Florida

$73.5K/year saved vs. Okta pricing

The situation: 1,040 users needed SSO and MFA for compliance. Okta quoted $75K/year.

What they got: FusionAuth implementation with full SSO and MFA coverage. SAML integration with their applications. User provisioning. The whole nine yards.

The outcome: $1,500/year instead of $75K. Same security posture. Same compliance checkboxes. $73.5K annual savings - every year, forever.

The math: Okta charges ~$6/user/month for SSO + MFA. At 1,040 users, that's $74,880/year. FusionAuth self-hosted with a small support plan? $1,500/year. Same features, 98% cost reduction.

WHAT I IMPLEMENT

Initial Setup & Configuration

Infrastructure setup (cloud or on-prem), tenant configuration, theming to match your brand, and initial security hardening.

SSO Integration

SAML and OIDC connections to your applications. Both off-the-shelf SaaS and custom-built apps. One login for everything.

MFA Configuration

TOTP (authenticator apps), SMS, email, and push notification options. Configurable policies for who needs MFA and when.

User Provisioning

Automated user creation and deprovisioning. SCIM integration if your apps support it. Directory sync with AD or Google Workspace.

Migration

Moving from another identity provider? I handle the migration - user data, password hashes where possible, minimal disruption.
