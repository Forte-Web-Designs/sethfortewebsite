WHEN ZAPIER HITS ITS LIMITS

Zapier is great until it isn't. Here's when we might need to look at other options:

Cost at scale. Task-based pricing adds up. A high-volume workflow can cost $200-500/month. At some point, Make.com or n8n makes more financial sense.

Complex logic. Paths help, but deeply nested conditionals get messy. If your workflow looks like a decision tree from hell, there might be a better way.

Custom integrations. 6,000+ apps is great, but if your tool isn't supported, Zapier's Webhooks and API Request actions have limits compared to other platforms.

I'll tell you honestly if Zapier isn't the right tool. Sometimes the answer is "use Make.com for this part and Zapier for that part." Sometimes it's "you need custom development." The goal is solving your problem, not forcing a platform.
