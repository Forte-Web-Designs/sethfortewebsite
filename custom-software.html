WHEN OFF-THE-SHELF DOESN'T FIT

You're bending your process to fit the software instead of the other way around. The tool that was supposed to make things easier now creates its own set of problems.

The 80% problem. The software does 80% of what you need perfectly. The other 20% is a constant workaround. Your team has developed elaborate processes just to bridge the gaps.

Spreadsheet chaos. The "real" data lives in a spreadsheet that someone updates manually. It's the source of truth because the official tool can't handle your actual workflow.

Feature requests go nowhere. You've submitted the feature request. It's been "under consideration" for two years. Your business can't wait for a vendor's product roadmap.

WHEN CUSTOM MAKES SENSE

Custom software isn't always the answer. Sometimes you just need the right off-the-shelf tool configured properly. Sometimes you need better processes, not better software. I'll tell you which.

Custom makes sense when:

  • Your workflow is genuinely unique to your business
  • Off-the-shelf tools create more problems than they solve
  • The ROI justifies the build (we'll calculate this together)
  • You need something simple that works exactly right

The technical build is the easy part. What you're paying for is someone who understands your business, builds for where you're going, and turns down projects that don't make sense.
