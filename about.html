The Longer Version

I spent years doing work that looked right on paper but didn't feel right.

Enterprise infrastructure. Millions of transactions. The kind of work where you learn to think three steps ahead because things can't afford to break. I was good at it. But I wasn't lit up.

I think everyone has something they were made to do. Not in a mystical way... more in a quiet, practical way. Like there's a version of your day that fits, and you can feel it when you're close to it.

So I started asking a different question. Not what looks good on paper, not what sounds impressive at dinner. What did I actually want? What made me come alive?

Turns out it was this. Taking something barely working: a CRM nobody configured, a follow up process in someone's head, five tools that don't talk. Rebuilding it so it runs itself.

I started Launch Forte on the side. Nights, weekends, whatever hours I could find. No business plan. No network. Seven years later, 60+ systems built across 12 industries. Still learning, still doing it.

What I've Figured Out So Far

I like understanding how things work. Businesses, processes, people. Everything's a system if you look at it right.

Most problems don't need sophisticated solutions. They need clear thinking and simple execution. Complexity is usually just untangled confusion.

I'd rather do fewer things well than more things badly.

I care whether the thing I build still works six months from now. Most people in this space don't stick around to find out.
