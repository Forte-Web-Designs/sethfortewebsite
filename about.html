About

I'm Seth. I live in Grapevine, Texas.

I spent five years at Wells Fargo building systems that couldn't afford to break. Enterprise stuff. Millions of transactions. The kind of work where you learn to think about edge cases before they become problems.

Now I build automation and custom software for smaller businesses. Different scale, same principles. Make it reliable. Make it understandable. Make it so someone else can maintain it when you're gone.

how I think about work:

I'd rather do fewer things well than more things badly.

I'm skeptical of complexity. Most problems don't need sophisticated solutions. They need clear thinking and simple execution.

I care whether the thing I build still works six months from now. A lot of consultants don't stick around long enough to find out. I do.

what I'm drawn to:

Problems where the real issue isn't obvious at first. Messy systems that need untangling. Businesses that have outgrown their tools but aren't sure what comes next.

I like working with people who are honest about what they don't know. Makes the work better.

Outside of work, I read a lot. Think about systems - not just software, but how things connect and influence each other. Try to get better at explaining complicated things simply.

Still figuring most of it out.
