N8N VS MAKE.COM VS ZAPIER: WHICH SHOULD YOU USE?

I've built production systems with all three. Here's the honest answer - not the SEO-optimized, hedge-everything version.

THE SHORT VERSION

Use Zapier if: You need simple triggers between common apps and want the easiest setup.

Use Make.com if: You need complex logic, branching workflows, or better pricing at scale.

Use n8n if: You want full control, self-hosting, or need to build something truly custom.

Skip all three if: Your problem is a broken process, not missing automation. No tool fixes bad workflows.

ZAPIER

What It Actually Is

The automation platform everyone knows. Connect App A to App B with triggers and actions. 6,000+ app integrations. The largest ecosystem by far.

Where It Shines

Simple, reliable connections. "When I get a new lead in Typeform, add them to my CRM and send a Slack notification." Zapier handles this perfectly.

Huge app library. If an app exists, Zapier probably connects to it. This matters when you're working with niche tools.

Tables and Interfaces. Their newer features let you build simple databases and forms. Good for lightweight internal tools.

Where It Struggles

Complex logic. Branching, loops, error handling - it gets messy fast. Paths help but aren't elegant.

Cost at scale. Task-based pricing adds up. A high-volume workflow can cost $200-500/month easily.

Debugging. When something breaks in a complex Zap, finding the issue is painful.

Real Cost

Starts at $19.99/month. But the real cost is task consumption. A single workflow that fires 1,000 times costs more than a simple one that fires 50 times. Budget for what you'll actually use, not the sticker price.

Best For

Small businesses with straightforward automation needs. Marketing teams connecting their stack. Anyone who values "it just works" over maximum flexibility.

MAKE.COM

What It Actually Is

Visual workflow builder with more power than Zapier. Formerly Integromat. Think of it as the middle ground - more flexible than Zapier, more approachable than n8n.

Where It Shines

Visual scenario builder. You can see exactly how data flows. Complex logic is easier to understand and debug.

Operations-based pricing. Often 5-10x cheaper than Zapier for the same workflow. The math works better at scale.

Advanced features. Routers, iterators, aggregators - you can build genuinely complex automations without code.

Where It Struggles

Smaller app library. Good coverage of major apps, but you'll hit gaps with niche tools.

Learning curve. More powerful means more to learn. Non-technical users need more ramp-up time.

Scenario complexity. Complex scenarios can become visual spaghetti. Organization matters.

Real Cost

Free tier available. Paid plans from $9/month. But again - budget for operations used, not the base price. Still significantly cheaper than Zapier for most use cases.

Best For

Businesses that outgrew Zapier's simplicity or pricing. Teams with someone technical enough to learn the interface. Complex workflows that need visual debugging.

N8N

What It Actually Is

Open-source workflow automation. Self-host it or use their cloud. The developer's choice - maximum flexibility, maximum control.

Where It Shines

Self-hosting option. Run it on your own servers. No per-execution fees. Full data control. This matters for compliance-heavy industries.

Custom code nodes. When you need to do something the platform doesn't support, write JavaScript. No limits.

Complex logic. Loops, conditionals, error handling - all first-class citizens. Build anything you can imagine.

Where It Struggles

Requires technical skills. Self-hosting means managing servers. Debugging means reading logs. This isn't for non-technical teams.

Fewer native integrations. You'll build more custom connections. HTTP requests become your friend.

Maintenance overhead. Updates, backups, monitoring - it's your responsibility when self-hosting.

Real Cost

Self-hosted: Free (plus your server costs, typically $20-100/month). Cloud: From $20/month. The hidden cost is technical expertise to run it properly.

Best For

Technical teams building complex systems. Businesses with compliance requirements. Anyone who needs custom logic that off-the-shelf tools can't handle.

WHAT I ACTUALLY USE

Depends on the project. Here's the reality from actual client work:

n8n - For complex systems that need custom logic. The KlnBros emergency recovery was an n8n infrastructure. When I'm building AI pipelines or need tight control over data flow, n8n wins.

Make.com - For visual workflows that clients need to understand. When someone's going to maintain this after I leave, Make's visual builder makes handoff easier.

Zapier - For simple connections where reliability matters more than cost optimization. Sometimes the boring choice is the right one.

Direct API integrations - When none of these fit. Sometimes you need custom code, not another platform. The Revolut analysis system was built this way - AI systems often need more control than any platform provides.

The platform is 20% of the decision. The other 80% is understanding the problem well enough to know what tool fits.

WHEN TO HIRE SOMEONE

Build it yourself if:

  • It's a simple connection between 2-3 apps
  • You have time to learn the platform
  • The stakes are low if it breaks

Hire help if:

  • You've tried and hit a wall
  • It's complex (multiple branches, error handling, data transformation)
  • Your time is worth more than the consultant's fee
  • It needs to work reliably - this isn't a side project

I turn down projects that don't make sense. If you're asking whether to build with Zapier vs. hire someone to do it - the honest answer is often "start with Zapier yourself and call me when you hit the limits."
