Automation ROI Calculator

The math is simple: if automation costs less than the problem, do it. Here's how to calculate it.

The Basic Formula

Annual Cost of Manual Work:

(Hours per week) x (Hourly cost) x 52 weeks = Annual Cost

Example: A task takes 5 hours/week at $30/hour fully-loaded cost.

5 x $30 x 52 = $7,800/year

What to Include in "Cost"

Direct Labor Cost

Salary + benefits + taxes. Rule of thumb: multiply salary by 1.3-1.4 for true cost.

Error Cost

What do mistakes cost? Wrong invoices, missed leads, incorrect data. Often overlooked.

Opportunity Cost

What could that person be doing instead? A $30/hour employee doing data entry can't do $100/hour sales work.

Speed Cost

How much does delay cost? Late follow-ups lose deals. Slow invoices hurt cash flow.

Real Examples

Data Entry Automation

Manual cost: 2 hours/day x $25/hour x 250 days = $12,500/year

Automation cost: $3,000 to build

Payback: 2.9 months

Year 1 savings: $9,500

Monthly Reporting

Manual cost: 4 hours/month x $40/hour x 12 months = $1,920/year

Automation cost: $2,500 to build

Payback: 15.6 months

Still worth it? Yes - it runs forever, and errors cost more than the math shows.

Lead Follow-up

Manual cost: Hard to calculate directly

Real cost: If slow response loses 10% of leads, and average deal is $5,000, and you get 50 leads/month...

5 lost deals x $5,000 = $25,000/month in lost revenue

Automation cost: $4,000

Worth it? Obviously.

When the Math Doesn't Work

Not every automation makes sense:

  • Low frequency: A task that happens once a quarter isn't worth automating
  • High complexity: If automation costs $50K and saves $10K/year, think hard
  • Changing process: Don't automate something you'll change in 6 months
  • Human judgment needed: Some things require a person

My Rule of Thumb

Most of my projects pay for themselves in 4-8 weeks. If the math doesn't show payback within 6 months, I'll tell you. Sometimes the answer is "this isn't worth automating."

The best automation investments are high-frequency, low-complexity tasks that someone is currently doing manually every day.
