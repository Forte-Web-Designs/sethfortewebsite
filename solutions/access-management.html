SSO & Access Management

Your team uses 15 different tools. Each has its own login. Someone leaves the company, and you spend a week tracking down all the accounts that need to be disabled. There's a better way.

The Problem

Password chaos - Different login for every tool. People reuse passwords, write them down, or can't get in when they need to.

Access creep - Permissions get added but never removed. Former employees still have access to things. Nobody knows who can see what.

No visibility - You can't answer basic questions. Who accessed that system? When? What did they do?

Compliance gaps - Auditors ask for access logs. You spend days manually compiling them from different systems.

What I Build

Single Sign-On (SSO)

One login for everything. Employee authenticates once, gets access to all their tools. Simple for users, controllable for admins.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Second layer of security across all systems. Not just the ones that make it easy.

Role-Based Access

Permissions tied to roles, not individuals. New hire gets the right access automatically. Departing employee loses it instantly.

Audit Trails

Who accessed what, when. Automatic logging. Compliance reports that generate themselves.

Real Example

San Florida needed SSO and MFA implementation for 1,040 users across multiple locations. Multiple applications, different user types, compliance requirements.

I evaluated options, implemented the solution with a phased rollout, and delivered enterprise-grade access management with zero downtime.

The "Enterprise" Misconception

People think proper access management requires enterprise budgets and IT teams.

It doesn't. The tools have gotten better and cheaper. What used to cost $50K and require dedicated staff can now be implemented for a fraction of that.

The question isn't whether you can afford it. It's whether you can afford not to have it.

When This Makes Sense

Growing team - More than 10 people. The manual approach stops scaling.

Sensitive data - Customer information, financial data, intellectual property. Things that matter if they leak.

Compliance requirements - HIPAA, SOC 2, client security questionnaires. Someone's asking about your access controls.

Turnover - People join and leave. You need onboarding and offboarding that isn't a scramble.

Investment

SSO and access management projects typically range from $5,000 to $15,000 depending on number of systems, user count, and compliance requirements.

The ROI includes: time saved on access management, reduced security risk, compliance audit costs avoided, and the thing you can't quantify - peace of mind.
