CRM Cleanup & Data Silos

Your CRM says one thing. The spreadsheet says another. Someone's personal notes say something else entirely. Sound familiar?

The Real Problem

It's not that you have bad data. It's that you have data everywhere - and none of it agrees.

Sales tracks leads in HubSpot. Marketing has their own spreadsheet. Finance pulls from QuickBooks. Customer success lives in Notion. And somewhere there's a Google Sheet that "has the real numbers" that someone updates manually every week.

This isn't a technology problem. It's a trust problem. When people don't trust the system, they build their own. And now you have ten systems instead of one.

What I Actually Fix

Duplicate Records

The same customer exists 4 times with slightly different spellings. Merge them properly without losing history.

Missing Data

Fields that should be populated aren't. Build systems that capture data automatically instead of relying on humans to remember.

Stale Data

Information that was accurate 18 months ago. Create processes that keep things current.

Disconnected Systems

CRM doesn't talk to accounting doesn't talk to support. Connect them so data flows automatically.

Shadow Spreadsheets

The "real" data lives outside the official system. Figure out why, fix the root cause, retire the workarounds.

The Goal

One source of truth.

Ask a question about a customer, get a reliable answer. From one place. That everyone trusts.

That's it. Simple to say, hard to do. But when it's done, everything else gets easier.

How This Works

Audit - Where does data live? Where does it come from? Where does it go? Map the reality, not the theory.

Clean - Deduplicate, standardize, fill gaps. The unsexy work that makes everything else possible.

Connect - Build integrations so data flows automatically between systems. No more manual syncing.

Sustain - Create processes that keep data clean going forward. A one-time cleanup that gets dirty again is useless.

Investment

CRM cleanup and integration projects typically range from $3,000 to $12,000 depending on the mess. Larger organizations with years of accumulated data debt are on the higher end.

The ROI? Hard to quantify, easy to feel. Decisions get faster. Mistakes go down. The weekly "reconciliation" meeting disappears.
